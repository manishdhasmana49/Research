Riding the Storm

Climate Change and the Waning Resilience of India's Railways

A Lifeline Under Threat

The Indian Railway Network (IRN) is the backbone of the nation's economy and mobility, moving over 100 billion passenger-kilometers annually. However, this vital lifeline is facing an unprecedented threat from climate change-induced extreme rainfall, leading to recurrent, costly, and dangerous flood-induced failures.

The Shifting Landscape of Flood Risk

A comparative analysis of two periods, 1901-1960 and 1961-2022, reveals a dramatic increase in flood hazards across the railway network. Many stations have transitioned to higher-risk categories, amplifying their vulnerability.

A Network Under Strain: Measuring Resilience

Resilience measures the network's ability to recover after a disruption. Our analysis shows a significant decline in the IRN's resilience to flooding in recent decades, coupled with a staggering rise in the economic value exposed to these failures.

Case Study: The July 2023 Northern India Floods

The devastating floods in Northern India in July 2023 serve as a stark example of these amplified risks. The event was a high-return-period flood, made more probable by the changing climate, and its impact on the Northern Railways was immediate and severe.
